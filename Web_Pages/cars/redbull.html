Overview

The Red Bull RB22 is arguably the most significant car in the team's history. Entering the 2026 regulatory era, Red Bull has officially become a fully independent works team, building their own engine in Milton Keynes through Red Bull Powertrains (RBPT) in partnership with Ford. Combining their legendary aerodynamic prowess with an entirely bespoke power unit, the RB22 aims to re-establish Red Bull dominance under the new active aero and 50/50 electrical regulations.

Technical Deep Dive

üîã Red Bull Powertrains (RBPT) x Ford

For the first time, the heart of the Red Bull beats in Milton Keynes. Taking on the massive challenge of building an F1 engine from scratch, Red Bull partnered with American giant Ford to tackle the 2026 hybrid rules. With the MGU-H banned, Ford's expertise was critical in developing the massively upscaled 350kW MGU-K and the sophisticated software required to deploy that electrical energy without "clipping" at high speeds.

‚öôÔ∏è Aerodynamics & Active Balance

Adapting to a narrower chassis and shorter wheelbase, the RB22's aerodynamic philosophy centers on the flawless transition of active aero states. The team has heavily focused its wind tunnel time on ensuring that when the front and rear wings shift from the low-drag X-Mode to the high-downforce Z-Mode under braking, the center of pressure remains perfectly stable for Max Verstappen's aggressive corner-entry style.

Red Bull's design philosophy for the RB22 is one of total independence. No longer reliant on Renault or Honda, the team has absorbed immense risk for the ultimate reward: total synergy.
