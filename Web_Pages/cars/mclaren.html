üîã Mercedes-AMG Partnership

While teams like Aston Martin abandoned their customer deals, McLaren doubled down on their Mercedes-AMG partnership for the 2026 regulations. The massive increase in electrical deployment (now roughly 50% of the total output) required McLaren's packaging department to radically rethink the MCL40's cooling architecture without compromising the aerodynamic surfaces.

‚öôÔ∏è Aerodynamics & Active Aero

The introduction of X-Mode and Z-Mode active aerodynamics fundamentally changes how the MCL40 attacks a lap. The Woking aerodynamicists focused heavily on the mechanical transition speed between the high-downforce cornering state and the low-drag straight-line state, ensuring Norris and Piastri maintain absolute confidence when hitting the braking zones.

With the 2026 rules forcing a shorter and narrower car, McLaren took the opportunity to optimize the center of gravity. The removal of the heavy MGU-H allowed them to redistribute the massive new battery packs lower into the floor, vastly improving the mechanical grip in low-speed traction zones.
