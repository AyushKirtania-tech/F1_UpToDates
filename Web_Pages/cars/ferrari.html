Technical Deep Dive

üîã Power Unit & Hybrid System

The SF-25 is powered by Ferrari's latest evolution of their turbocharged 1.6-liter V6 internal combustion engine. This power unit works in harmony with two Motor Generator Units (MGUs): the MGU-K recovers kinetic energy during braking, while the MGU-H harvests energy from exhaust gases through the turbocharger. Together, the system produces approximately 1,000 horsepower, with the hybrid components adding around 160 HP of electrical boost. The engine revs to 15,000 RPM and features direct fuel injection for optimal efficiency and power delivery.

‚öôÔ∏è Aerodynamics & Downforce

The SF-25 features a completely redesigned aerodynamic package compared to its predecessor. The front wing has been optimized for better airflow management, while the floor and diffuser have been extensively reworked to maximize ground effect downforce. The rear wing features a DRS (Drag Reduction System) that can be deployed in designated zones for increased straight-line speed. The bodywork is sculpted to minimize drag while maximizing cooling efficiency for the power unit and brakes. At high speeds, the car can generate up to 5 times its own weight in downforce.

üèóÔ∏è Chassis & Construction

Built around a carbon-fiber composite monocoque chassis with honeycomb structures, the SF-25 achieves an optimal balance between strength, rigidity, and weight. The chassis must meet the FIA's minimum weight requirement of 740 kg (including driver). Every component is engineered to save weight while maintaining structural integrity and safety. The survival cell protects the driver in the event of an impact, incorporating advanced crash structures that have been rigorously tested to exceed FIA safety standards.

The system features self-ventilated discs at both the front and rear, with an electronically controlled hydraulic braking system on the rear wheels that works in conjunction with the energy recovery system. Under heavy braking, the car can decelerate at forces exceeding 5G, with the carbon discs providing consistent performance lap after lap throughout a race distance.

üéØ Electronics & Control Systems

The SF-25's electronics are governed by a standardized FIA ECU, but Ferrari has optimized every parameter within the regulations. The car features advanced traction control algorithms, energy management systems for the hybrid components, and sophisticated brake-by-wire technology. The steering wheel serves as the driver's interface, featuring dozens of switches and rotary dials for adjusting everything from differential settings to energy deployment modes. Real-time telemetry allows the team to monitor hundreds of sensors throughout the car during every session.
