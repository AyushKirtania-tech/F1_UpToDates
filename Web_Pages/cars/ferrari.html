Technical Deep Dive

üîã The 50/50 Power Unit

The SF-26 features an all-new power unit dictated by the 2026 regulations. The complex MGU-H has been banned, replaced by a massively upscaled MGU-K system on the rear axle. The electrical deployment now accounts for nearly 50% of the car's total 1,000+ horsepower. Ferrari's engine department has focused extensively on harvesting efficiency and battery deployment strategies to prevent electrical "clipping" on long straights.

‚öôÔ∏è X-Mode and Z-Mode Aero

For the first time in F1 history, the SF-26 features active aerodynamics on both the front and rear wings. Downstraights, the driver engages "X-Mode," stalling the wings to drastically reduce drag and battery consumption. In cornering, "Z-Mode" snaps the flaps into a high-downforce configuration. Mastering the mechanical transition between these two states was Maranello's primary focus in the wind tunnel.
